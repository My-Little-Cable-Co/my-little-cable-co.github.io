What is this?

This is a resource for creating your own personal little cable company.

Why would anyone do that?

For fun, mostly. But also because media you don't control will always seek to control you.

TV entertains, and TV influences. TV connects us with a common culture. What if you reject that culture, and the influence that comes with it? Whether it is traditional cable service, video sharing websites, or social media, we are not in complete control over what content gets pushed to us. The channels show you what the media corportation decided you should see. The video sharing sites and social media sites are incentivised to keep you watching. They don't care what you watch, only that you contribute to their never-ending organizational goal of "people watch more". This has resulted in the indoctrination of thousands, as the automated recommendations trend towards the extremes of topics.

Even if you manage to avoid extremist content, you are still likely benefiting the authors of said content. In the case of bundled content, like traditional cable services, even if you are morally opposed to a channel's content, a portion of your bill helps fund it. Every month. This applies to newer, streaming-based live TV services as well. If you are able to watch a channel, you are paying for that channel, and supporting everything they support.

By curating your own content and providing your own service, you can avoid the pitfalls that come with modern media. Or... maybe you just want to play with some new-to-you technology and create your own channels with their own personas.

How do you do it?

Like many hobbies, there are varying degrees of how "into it" you get. It all depends on how much time and money you are willing to put into it, and where you begin to start seeing diminishing returns. We'll outline several different setups along with the equipment and costs involved. Any of these approaches are valid if they fit your money/effort budget.

Big Picture Objective, Regardless of Project Scale

Whether you have a single channel displayed on a computer or dozens of channels available to TVs throughout your house, your core objective is simple: have one or more video feeds of scheduled content delivered to a display.

Scenario 1: Simple

Objective: Schedule Video Files to Play at Specific Times

Suggested Equipment:

  • A computer
  • Video files

Suggested Budget, assuming you have a computer: $0.00

Scenario 2: Advanced

Objective: Have multiple channels to flip between on a real TV, including a guide channel.

Suggested Equipment:

  • A multi-input RF modulator
  • A TV capable of tuning analog signals
  • One Raspberry Pi capable of outputting composite video for each input on the RF modulator
  • Video files

Suggested Budget: $200-300

Click here to get started!

Scenario 3: Enthusiast

Objective: Have an expandable system with many channels, received by multiple TVs throughout your living space.

Suggested Equipment:

  • One channelized or agile modulator per desired channel
  • Enough signal combiners to merge the channel signals into one
  • A distribution amplifier
  • One Raspberry Pi capable of outputting composite video for each modulator
  • A network attached storage device
  • A rack mount network switch
  • An equipment rack to hold all of this
  • Multiple TVs capable of tuning analog signals
  • Video files
